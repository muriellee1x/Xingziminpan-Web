ENGLISH TRANSLATION

From the Dao, one gives rise to three,
From three to all, life flows like a stream.
The great bird's flight begins in the North,
On this pilgrim's path, where lies my worth?

I knew not the realms of spirit and flesh,
Nor could I discern the self from the mesh.
The road behind me, choked by the weeds,
In fate's rushing river, my spirit bleeds.

I once questioned heaven and earth in vain,
Yet silence answered, leaving only pain.
Winds and rains pass in a hurried embrace,
Mountains tremble, all time laid to waste.

Who does the light shine for, and where?
In this endless world, I pause in despair.
Remember this moment, fleeting and rare,
The unknown awaits; we'll meet it with care.

Like grains of sand in the Ganges, they flow,
In life's tangled web, shadows ebb and glow.

I long to turn back, yet fall into night,
In the swirling abyss, I struggle for light.

I pray to the sun as dark clouds conspire,
I call to the moon, where are the stars' fire?

To glance at the past could harden my soul,
To seek out the echoes leaves me unwhole.

I yearn to trace rivers that once flowed bright,
I long for the paths, but answers take flight.

Where lies the way ahead? I see no path ahead
Where lies the way ahead? I see no path ahead
